Imagine if you were so dedicated to academics like you were your favourite competitive game, and ignore diminshing returns
I used to play rocket league, and as an example as a beginner I would spend 10 hours or so to improve enough to
advance to the next rank. However, after a cycle of that, the time I invested wouldn't be that valuable anymore.
When I was at the top level, it would take me 100 hours to advance to the next level
But I was so dedicated, out of motivation and perhaps addiction, and also pure enjoyment of chasing things,
that I wouldn't mind and I would spend 200 hours to improve 0.000002% just to have a chance of getting to the
next rank. Of course people learn at different speeds, but you only get better at learning by learning more

Your first language you learn is easy because you don't realise the effort you put in and subconsciously
put thousands upon thousands of hours in. The second language you learn is hard because now you
not only need to learn how to learn, but as soon as you pass the beginner stage all your time is
spent getting less returns than you did first starting. It's the same idea with going to the gym,
noobie gains are insane and you will see the biggest difference in hypertrophy at the beginning,
but afterwards you'll be chasing 0.1% increases in muscle mass. Perhaps not the best two examples to finish on,
as neither of them are addicting or fun, but you get the idea of diminishing returns.
